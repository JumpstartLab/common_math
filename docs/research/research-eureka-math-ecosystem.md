# Eureka Math / EngageNY Third-Party Ecosystem Research

## Background and Scale

Eureka Math was developed by the nonprofit Great Minds (founded 2007 by Lynne Munson) under a 2012 contract with the New York State Education Department. That original OER version was called EngageNY Math. Great Minds later updated and commercialized it as Eureka Math, which is now the most widely used K-5 math program in the United States. According to RAND Corporation surveys, it's the top-adopted elementary math curriculum nationally. The original EngageNY curriculum has been downloaded over 13 million times across all 50 states. Great Minds released a substantially revised version called Eureka Math Squared (sometimes written Eureka Math²) starting around 2021-2022.

The OER/EngageNY version is licensed under Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike (CC BY-NC-SA), though NYSED discontinued the EngageNY.org website in July 2022. The commercial Eureka Math is still downloadable as PDF for free noncommercial use, but printed materials and digital tools require purchase.

## Third-Party Support Materials

### 1. Teachers Pay Teachers (TPT)

TPT is the primary marketplace for Eureka Math supplemental content, with thousands of products spanning every grade level and module. The demand is driven by two widely-acknowledged problems: the curriculum is hard to pace, and students frequently need additional practice at a more accessible level than what the curriculum provides natively.

**Types of materials sold:**
- Module-by-module supplemental practice pages (simpler than the official curriculum's practice sets)
- Exit tickets aligned to specific lessons
- Parent-facing review guides for homework nights
- Sprint alternatives and fluency practice
- Centers and games aligned to specific modules
- Application problem practice sets
- Mid-module and end-of-module assessment review bundles
- Anchor charts and vocabulary wall cards
- Full module bundles (topic A through F or similar)
- Digital versions (Google Slides, Google Forms) of practice materials

**Notable sellers:**
- **Spectacular in Second** — One of the better-known TPT sellers specifically for Eureka Math supplements. They produce simplified practice pages explicitly designed for students who struggle with the complexity of the official curriculum's worksheets. Described in teacher communities as a go-to supplement to buy alongside each module.
- **Eureka Math Teaching Resources** — An official TPT store under that name exists, though it is unclear whether this is affiliated with Great Minds or is a third-party seller using the name.
- Many individual teachers have built stores around grade-specific Eureka content. The search volume on TPT for "eureka math" is substantial, with separate high-traffic searches for "eureka math grade 3," "eureka math supplemental," "eureka math curriculum," "eureka math squared," etc.

**Business scale:** TPT seller revenue for Eureka Math content is not publicly reported, but given the curriculum's national adoption and the recurring need for supplemental practice, this is a meaningful niche. Sellers producing full grade-level bundles can price them at $20-60+ per grade-level bundle, and a seller covering multiple grades with multiple module bundles can accumulate significant catalog value.

**What's missing on TPT:** High-quality parent-facing explanatory materials remain underdeveloped. Most TPT products target teachers, not parents trying to help with homework at home.

### 2. EMBARC.online

EMBARC stands for **Eureka Math Bay Area Regional Consortium**. It was originally created to support local Bay Area teachers using Eureka Math, and has grown into a nationally significant free platform, now serving **30,000 users per day** across the country.

**What EMBARC offers:**
- Ready-to-use interactive lessons organized by grade and module
- Google Slides presentations for lessons
- Topic quizzes for grades 1-5, converted to Google Docs for easy teacher customization
- Module assessments reformatted as Google Docs
- Lesson-specific guides and supplemental activities
- Curated links to third-party resources that enhance specific Eureka lessons
- Pacing guidance and advice on adapting the curriculum
- Resources organized from Kindergarten through 12th grade, though coverage is stronger in elementary

**Platform:** EMBARC runs on Moodle (an open-source LMS). Content is organized hierarchically: Grade > Module > Topic > Lesson. This makes it easy to navigate to specific lessons.

**Community:** EMBARC functions as a collaborative, community-maintained resource. Educators contribute materials. It is free and does not appear to charge users.

**Significance:** EMBARC is arguably the most important third-party resource in the Eureka ecosystem — a free, comprehensive, lesson-level resource that essentially serves as a better-organized companion to the official curriculum. Its 30,000 daily users is a meaningful signal of how underserved teachers feel by official materials alone.

### 3. Zearn

Zearn is a nonprofit digital math platform that is closely aligned with Eureka Math. It was co-founded in 2012 by CEO Shalinee Sharma and Evan Rudall (former CEO of Uncommon Schools).

**Relationship to Eureka Math:**
- Zearn Math K-5 lessons directly follow the scope and sequence of Eureka Math/EngageNY
- Middle school materials align to Eureka Math/EngageNY on the unit level and can be reordered to match the curriculum
- Zearn also produces alignment documents specifically mapping to Eureka Math Squared
- Zearn has a formal licensing relationship: "Portions of Zearn Math are derivative of Eureka Math and licensed by Great Minds, © 2019 Great Minds, Inc."

**What Zearn offers:**
- 450+ hours of interactive digital lessons for grades K-8
- Adaptive daily digital lessons with warm-up activities
- Guided video lessons that follow a Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract (CPA) progression
- Mastery-based closing quizzes
- Teacher dashboard with student progress tracking
- The core instructional content is **free** for students and teachers
- Paid tier for districts/schools provides implementation support and additional features

**Scale:**
- As of 2022, used by approximately 25% of US elementary school students
- Over 1 million middle school student users
- More than 60% of school partners are Title I schools (as of February 2025)
- Revenue was $46M for the year ending June 2022, growing at ~54% annually
- Received $4.4M from the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation
- Evidence for ESSA rates their digital lessons as "Strong"
- Colorado used $9M in federal pandemic relief funds to provide free Zearn access statewide

**Significance:** Zearn is the dominant digital companion to Eureka Math. For a teacher or parent wanting digital practice that mirrors the classroom curriculum, Zearn is the primary option and it is largely free. This is a very significant part of the ecosystem.

### 4. Duane Habecker / YouTube

Duane Habecker is a math teacher who built a comprehensive video library covering every lesson in the Eureka Math/EngageNY curriculum. His videos are widely cited and linked by school districts across the country on parent resource pages.

**What he offers:**
- Videos for every grade level, every module, and every lesson
- Each video is approximately 10 minutes long
- Videos walk through the lesson content, making them suitable for students to watch as homework help, for parents to understand how to help their children, and for teachers to preview lessons
- Available on YouTube at youtube.com/user/dhabecker
- Connected to EMBARC.online (he is associated with EMBARC's origins)

**Reach:** His videos are embedded or linked on dozens of school district websites, teacher resource pages, and parent guides. His Twitter handle is @dhabecker. The exact subscriber count was not readily available in public search results, but the depth of his coverage (every grade, module, and lesson) makes his channel uniquely comprehensive — no other single creator appears to have matched this scope.

**Significance:** Duane Habecker's videos function as a de facto video companion to the Eureka Math curriculum. Many schools point parents to his channel as the primary resource for understanding homework. This is a significant gap that was filled entirely by a single independent teacher.

### 5. Khan Academy

Khan Academy has built out an EngageNY/Eureka Math-aligned track within their platform.

**What they offer:**
- Full course sequences for grades K-8 organized by EngageNY/Eureka Math module and unit structure
- Practice exercises and video lessons
- Courses include: 3rd grade foundations (Eureka Math/EngageNY), 6th grade (Eureka Math/EngageNY), 8th grade (Eureka Math/EngageNY), and others
- Remediation guides specifically for Eureka Math teachers (e.g., "Remediation and Eureka Math/EngageNY")
- Documentation for teachers on how to use Khan alongside Eureka Math

**Relationship:** Khan Academy describes these as "on-grade support" for Eureka Math/EngageNY. The content is organized to mirror the Eureka scope and sequence, so teachers can assign Khan exercises as supplementary practice aligned to current classroom lessons.

**Free:** All Khan Academy content is free.

### 6. GeoGebra

GeoGebra, the free interactive math tools platform used by 100+ million students and teachers globally, has an Eureka Math/EngageNY-specific collection.

**What exists:**
- An organized collection of GeoGebra applets and interactives aligned to EngageNY/Eureka Math (at geogebra.org/m/ua4yNy75)
- EMBARC curates links to specific GeoGebra activities for particular lessons
- GeoGebra is frequently mentioned by Eureka Math teachers as a supplement for interactive exploration

Great Minds' FAQ notes that while they do not offer their own suite of interactive tools, they point to GeoGebra as a recommended third-party resource.

### 7. Other Notable Content Creators and Platforms

**Mary Iverstine (maryiverstine.com):** A blogger who has written extensively about parent resources for Eureka Math, including a "Top 5 Parent Resources" post that circulates widely. Creates guidance for parents navigating the curriculum.

**Common Core Café (commoncorecafe.blogspot.com):** A blog that compiled parent resources for all grade levels, widely linked by school districts.

**AK Teacher Life / Teaching at the Oaks:** Teachers who have blogged about implementing Eureka Math and created resource guides.

**edtech4aleks.com:** Maintains a K-8 scope and sequence using Eureka Math modules with ALEKS alignment notes.

**Better Together Family Therapy:** A therapy practice that wrote parent guides specifically about helping children with Eureka Math homework — this exists because the parent frustration level is high enough to generate demand for this kind of support content from unlikely sources.

**Proteacher.net:** A teacher forum (not Reddit) with active Eureka Math threads. Multiple threads on topics including "Eureka Math struggles," "Ditching my math curriculum," and "Eureka Math or Bridges?" indicate sustained community discussion going back years.

## Official Digital Products (Great Minds)

For completeness, the official Great Minds digital ecosystem:

- **Eureka Digital Suite / Navigator:** Interactive digital version of the PK-12 curriculum, plus Teach Eureka video series (professional development). Paid product.
- **Affirm:** Digital assessment and practice tool with 450+ assessments for Grade 1 through Precalculus. Integrates with Google Classroom, Canvas, and Clever. Announced 2020. Paid product.
- **Eureka Math Equip:** Diagnostic tool for identifying prerequisite knowledge gaps before modules begin. Paid product.
- **Eureka Math In Sync:** Additional digital resource. Paid product.
- **Eureka Math Squared digital platform:** The new 2021+ version comes with a dedicated digital platform including presentation slides and digital interactives. Paid product.

**Key insight:** The core curriculum PDFs are free. All the digital tools, interactives, assessments, and enhancements are paid. This creates the gap that third parties like EMBARC and Zearn fill for free.

## Physical Manipulatives

**Didax** is the **sole-source, exclusive provider** of official Eureka Math manipulatives. They partnered with Great Minds to identify and produce the specific hands-on materials needed for the curriculum.

- They sell grade-level complete manipulative kits, upgrade kits for districts transitioning from original Eureka Math to Eureka Math Squared, and over 75 individual items
- Kits for Eureka Math Squared are branded separately
- This is a meaningful business: a complete manipulative kit for a classroom of 24 is a significant purchase

## Apps and Homework Help Tools

**No dedicated third-party app exists** specifically for Eureka Math homework help. The ecosystem for mobile support is thin.

**What does exist:**
- **Zearn** has a student-facing web platform (not a native app) that works on tablets/chromebooks
- **Eureka: Fast Math Solver** (App Store, id6738960478) — This is an unrelated AI-powered math solver app that happens to share the name "Eureka." Not Eureka Math specific.
- **Great Minds' Homework Helpers** — These are printed/PDF spiral-bound books (one per homework assignment) with step-by-step worked examples similar to the homework problems. Available in English and Spanish, grades K-8. Free PDF download. These are the primary parent-facing tool, but they are static documents, not interactive.
- **Family Tip Sheets** — Free PDFs from Great Minds, topic-level, covering key vocabulary and strategies. English and Spanish, grades K-8.

**The gap:** There is no mobile app, chatbot, or interactive homework-help tool that says "I'm in Grade 3 Module 5 Lesson 12" and gives targeted help. The Homework Helpers PDFs require parents to know which lesson number they're on and navigate a PDF. This is a meaningful unmet need.

## Data and Structured Content

### Machine-Readable Curriculum Data

No publicly known machine-readable, structured dataset of Eureka Math's full scope and sequence exists as an open-source project. What does exist:

**Official PDFs only:** Great Minds publishes scope and sequence, curriculum maps, and pacing guides as PDF documents in their free Teacher Resource Pack. These include curriculum overviews, standards checklists, materials lists, and pacing guides — all PDF format, not structured data.

**TEKS Edition scope and sequence:** A Eureka Math TEKS Edition scope and sequence exists as a PDF (for the Texas standards version), hosted on HubSpot by Great Minds.

**edtech4aleks.com** maintains a K-8 scope and sequence page showing Eureka Math modules with ALEKS skill alignment, but this appears to be maintained manually and is not a structured dataset.

### Common Core Standards Alignment

**Official alignment studies:** Great Minds publishes PDF alignment studies for every state (all 50 states, D.C., and the U.K.), showing how Eureka Math and Eureka Math Squared map to state standards. These are comprehensive but in PDF format.

**Zearn alignment documents:** Zearn publishes specific PDF alignment matrices showing how Zearn Math maps to Eureka Math (original) and Eureka Math Squared at the lesson/unit level. These are more granular than the official Great Minds documents and are useful for cross-referencing.

**Khan Academy:** Has structured their EngageNY/Eureka tracks internally, but this isn't exposed as downloadable structured data.

**OER Commons:** The EngageNY collection at OER Commons (oercommons.org/curated-collections/127) contains lessons with CCSS metadata tags. OER Commons uses standardized metadata (including CCSS alignment tags) on individual resources, which is the closest thing to a machine-readable curriculum alignment database that exists publicly. The Achieve/ISKME tools on OER Commons allow CCSS-domain-level browsing.

### GitHub / Open Source

A search for Eureka Math-specific GitHub repositories found nothing relevant. The "Eureka" repositories on GitHub are unrelated projects (JWST data pipeline, AI reward design research, multimodal reasoning). No open-source project appears to have built:
- A machine-readable Eureka Math scope and sequence
- A structured lesson-to-standard mapping database
- A curriculum data API
- Any curriculum-related tool

The EngageNY documents are CC BY-NC-SA licensed, so the legal path for building such a dataset is open — it just hasn't been done publicly.

## Common Pain Points (Synthesized from Community Sources)

These themes recur consistently across ProTeacher threads, Reddit, blog posts, and press coverage:

**1. Pacing is nearly impossible**
The curriculum assumes more time than most schools allocate. Teachers consistently report that completing a full lesson in 60 minutes is not achievable. Districts that mandate fidelity to the program put teachers in a constant state of stress. EMBARC even has a dedicated pacing guidance section because this is so prevalent.

**2. Parents cannot help with homework**
The methods taught (tape diagrams, place value disks, area models, number bonds) are unfamiliar to most parents who learned traditional algorithms. School districts send home videos. Many schools host "Eureka Math Nights" to teach parents. Therapists write blog posts about stress from Eureka homework. This is a genuine, widespread problem with no great solution currently.

**3. Developmental appropriateness concerns**
Teachers in early grades (K-2) particularly note that the curriculum expects reading comprehension that isn't yet developmentally appropriate. First graders encounter multi-step word problems. The curriculum is academically rigorous in ways that can be a mismatch for some developmental stages.

**4. No differentiation support**
The curriculum is designed as a coherent whole — you can't easily skip components. Teachers with wide-ability classrooms struggle to adapt it for both struggling learners and advanced students. The supplemental market on TPT exists largely because of this gap.

**5. First-year implementation is brutal**
Nearly every teacher who discusses Eureka Math notes that year one requires enormous prep. Every lesson requires materials, deep content knowledge, and fluency with a specific teaching approach. Teachers in their first year with the curriculum describe spending hours on preparation for each lesson.

**6. Student materials are visually unappealing**
The original Eureka Math student-facing materials are text-heavy, have no color, no graphics, and low production values. Eureka Math Squared attempted to address this with updated design, but the original materials remain in wide use.

**7. Fluency activities are confusing**
The "sprint" activities (timed fluency exercises) are controversial — some teachers find them effective, others find them anxiety-inducing and confusing in their format.

## Key Gaps and Observations

1. **No dedicated homework-help app**: The clearest unmet need is a mobile or web tool that helps parents understand what their child is working on in Eureka Math, lesson by lesson, with explanations in plain language. Duane Habecker's YouTube videos partially fill this, but they require knowing to look for his channel and navigating YouTube.

2. **No structured curriculum data**: All curriculum scope/sequence information exists in PDF format only. No machine-readable dataset maps Eureka Math lessons to CCSS standards in a format consumable by software.

3. **Parent confusion is endemic and underserved**: There is significant demand for parent-facing content (Mary Iverstine's blog, therapy practice guides, district-produced help sheets) but no unified, well-designed parent companion product.

4. **TPT market is teacher-only**: Virtually all TPT Eureka Math content targets teachers. Parent-targeted products on TPT are minimal.

5. **EMBARC fills a real gap but is resource-poor**: A Moodle site maintained by educators with 30,000 daily users is doing a lot of work with, presumably, minimal funding. This is a meaningful infrastructure dependency.

6. **Zearn owns the digital practice space**: For any product wanting to compete in digital practice aligned to Eureka Math, Zearn is the incumbent with massive scale, ESSA evidence rating, free pricing, and established district relationships.

7. **The transition to Eureka Math Squared is creating fresh demand**: Districts transitioning from original Eureka to Eureka Math Squared need new supplemental materials, new alignment maps, and updated teacher support. TPT sellers and resource creators are only beginning to produce Eureka Math Squared-specific content.
