# Duane Habecker: Deep Research Profile

## Summary

Duane Habecker is a California math educator who, as a side project over many years, built what became the de facto video companion for the entire Eureka Math / EngageNY curriculum. He also co-founded EMBARC.online, which grew from a local Bay Area teacher consortium into a free national platform serving 30,000 users per day. His YouTube channel has ~133K subscribers, approximately 1,000+ videos, and an estimated 48 million total views. His work was done without any affiliation with Great Minds (Eureka's publisher) and appears to have been motivated almost entirely by a desire to serve his students and community. There is no evidence he sells anything or has commercialized any of this.

## The Person

### Career Timeline

Duane Habecker has been in mathematics education since 1989. His career has three phases:

**Classroom teacher (1989–2013):** Habecker taught mathematics for approximately 24 years, working in grades 6–9 across the Oakland, Hayward, and Pleasanton school systems in the San Francisco Bay Area. His most prominent classroom role was at Pleasanton Middle School, where he taught sixth, seventh, and eighth grade math. Even as a classroom teacher, he was an early adopter of digital tools — he was featured in *Education World* for building a "Math Web Site" for his students that provided homework resources, practice quizzes, and parent help tips.

**Instructional coach (2013–2017):** Habecker transitioned from the classroom to serve as one of eight mathematics instructional coaches in Pleasanton Unified School District. During this period, PUSD adopted Eureka Math, and Habecker's video work and EMBARC both appear to have developed substantially during this window. He presented the district's Common Core math implementation update to the school board in 2014.

**Math Coordinator, Merced County Office of Education (2017–present):** He moved from the Bay Area to central California to serve as Senior Mathematics Coordinator for MCOE, which provides educational services to 20 school districts. In this role he supports approximately 6,000 students across 12 of those districts, covering math instruction from preschool through AP calculus. He also serves as President of the California Mathematics Council's Central Region and presents regularly at national conferences including NCTM and NCSM.

### Background and Identity

- Born July 24, 1967; native of Pleasanton, California
- Has maintained a presence at dhabecker@gmail.com and on Twitter/Mastodon as @dhabecker
- Phone listed publicly via about.me: 925-400-8454 (a 925 area code is Pleasanton/Livermore)
- His website at theothermath.com describes his broader pedagogy philosophy; also maintains mathvillage.info

### Teaching Philosophy

A formative moment in college — when a professor showed him that despite his computational ability, he lacked deep mathematical understanding — shaped how Habecker thinks about math instruction. His central belief: "Math isn't about getting the right answer, it's about making sense of the world." He advocates for student-centered, discourse-driven instruction and has spoken extensively on tape diagrams, number talks, the "5 Practices" framework, and instructional equity including Universal Design for Learning. His broader platform at *The Other Math* frames his mission as "more than what's in the textbook."

### Relationship with Great Minds

There is no formal relationship. EMBARC explicitly states: "EMBARC is an independent organization and is not affiliated with, or sponsored or endorsed by, Great Minds." His videos are his own creation, produced independently. He has never been on Great Minds' payroll or listed as a partner.

## The YouTube Channel

### Scale and Statistics

The most recent cross-referenced data points (from tracker sites and search results, circa 2023–2024):

- **Subscribers:** ~116K–137K (figures vary by source and date; 133K appears in multiple 2024 references)
- **Total videos:** ~1,000–1,077
- **Total views:** Estimated 48 million (one source cited ~19.4M from an older tracking date; other sources suggest growth to ~48M)

These numbers place him in the small but real tier of educator YouTubers who have built significant audiences without celebrity or media backing.

### Content Coverage

Habecker's videos cover the Eureka Math / EngageNY curriculum at a depth unmatched by any other single creator:

- **Grades:** Kindergarten through Grade 8 (with some high school content)
- **Scope:** Every grade, every module, every lesson
- This "every lesson" completeness is what distinguishes his library. Most other creators pick and choose; Habecker did the whole thing.

The videos are organized as YouTube playlists by grade and module, making it straightforward to navigate to a specific lesson (e.g., "Grade 3 Module 5 Lesson 12").

**Important caveat:** As of 2022–2023, some schools note that updated versions of Eureka Math lessons no longer directly correlate with Habecker's module numbering, though the underlying concepts and strategies remain the same. His videos are aligned to the original Eureka Math / EngageNY version, not Eureka Math Squared (the 2021+ revision).

### Video Format and Production Quality

- **Format:** Screen-recorded interactive whiteboard videos, created using tools like Educreations (an iPad-based recordable whiteboard app). This is a "teacher in front of a digital whiteboard" style — handwritten, worked-through problems with audio narration.
- **Length:** Typically around 10 minutes per video. Earlier descriptions suggest some videos are shorter (2–4 minutes for older general math content); the Eureka Math lesson videos are generally longer.
- **Style:** He walks through the lesson's problems and strategies in real time, narrating as he goes. The approach mirrors what a teacher would do at the board during the actual lesson. It is not high-production-value studio work; it is functional and clear.
- **Production quality:** Described as having "quality and clean editing" by channel tracker Callmart. The consensus from teachers and parents is that the content is clear and sufficient — not cinematic, but effective. Teachers call it "a life saver."
- **No on-camera presence:** These are screen recordings with audio, not talking-head videos. This keeps them efficient and content-focused.

### Content Beyond Eureka Math

Habecker has created mathematical content beyond Eureka Math on multiple platforms:

- Early middle school math tutorials (pre-Eureka era, still on his channel)
- GeoGebra activities (24+ interactive resources covering fractions, geometry, coordinates, integer operations, puzzles, and blank whiteboard tools)
- Google Slides math games designed for remote/collaborative learning (highlighted during COVID-era)
- CK-12 Flexbooks (he created 15 Flexbooks combining his content with CK-12 materials during COVID school closures in 2020)
- Educreations lessons (33+ lessons focused on Grade 5 Module 1 content, with the top lesson having 25K+ views)

His other websites (The Other Math, MathVillage) offer pedagogical articles, puzzles, and games rather than lesson-by-lesson video instruction.

### How Teachers Use His Videos

Based on multiple teacher accounts:

1. **Personal professional development** — Teachers watch his videos to understand a lesson strategy they haven't taught before, or to preview a module they're unfamiliar with. This is especially useful for teachers who change grade levels mid-year (one teacher described this use case explicitly while moving from 3rd to 5th grade).

2. **Parent communication resource** — Teachers share specific video links when a parent asks how to help with homework. The videos show the specific Eureka Math strategies (tape diagrams, number bonds, place value charts) that differ from how parents learned math.

3. **Student homework help** — Schools send parents to his channel so students can watch the lesson being worked through if they missed something or got stuck.

4. **Catch-up resource** — Students who missed class can watch the relevant lesson video.

The recurring teacher phrase is "life saver" — particularly for explaining Eureka Math's distinctive pedagogical approach to parents who find it unfamiliar.

## EMBARC: Habecker's Bigger Project

### What EMBARC Is

EMBARC stands for **Eureka Math Bay Area Regional Consortium**. It is a free, nationally-used online platform for Eureka Math support materials, running on Moodle (an open-source LMS). It serves 30,000 users per day.

### Founding and Structure

EMBARC was founded by Duane Habecker and Rebecca Poon, both associated with Bay Area school districts during the period when those districts were adopting Eureka Math (roughly 2013–2016). The name reflects its origins: a consortium of Bay Area school districts — Pleasanton USD, Castro Valley USD, San Leandro USD, San Ramon USD, Kentfield SD, and Union School District (Santa Clara) — who pooled resources to support their Eureka implementations. The full consortium list suggests this began as a formal inter-district collaboration, not just a personal project.

From this local origin, EMBARC expanded to serve the national Eureka Math community. The Pleasanton Moodle instance (moodle.pleasantonusd.net) appears to have been an early or parallel hosting site before EMBARC.online became the primary public-facing platform.

### What EMBARC Offers

Resources organized by grade (K–8, with some high school) and module:

- Video lessons for every topic (these appear to include Habecker's YouTube videos embedded or linked)
- Homework solutions
- Topic quizzes formatted as Google Docs
- Module assessments as Google Docs
- Virtual manipulatives
- Reference sheets and teaching strategies
- Google Slides presentations for lessons
- Pacing guidance

**License:** All EMBARC content is released under Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0).

### EMBARC's Independence from Great Minds

EMBARC explicitly disclaims any affiliation with Great Minds. This is legally significant: Great Minds has been protective of the Eureka Math brand, and EMBARC's disclaimer ensures they operate in the OER/EngageNY lane rather than risking trademark issues.

### Scale Context

30,000 daily users is a meaningful number. For reference, that's roughly 7.5 million user-sessions per year from a platform run by a county math coordinator and a co-founder, with no evident paid staff. It is the dominant free comprehensive resource for Eureka Math teachers.

## License and Embedding Considerations

### YouTube License

Habecker's videos are almost certainly under the standard YouTube license (not Creative Commons). The standard YouTube license allows:
- Viewing on YouTube
- Embedding on other websites via YouTube's embed player
- Sharing links

It does NOT allow:
- Downloading and re-hosting
- Re-editing or re-distributing
- Commercial use without permission

EMBARC's own materials are under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0, but that license applies to EMBARC's resources (quizzes, Google Docs, etc.), not to the YouTube videos themselves, which are governed separately.

### Embedding in an App

Embedding YouTube videos in a web-based app using YouTube's standard iframe embed player is technically permissible under YouTube's Terms of Service, provided:
- The embed uses YouTube's player (not a downloaded/re-hosted copy)
- The context is not deceptive or harmful
- YouTube's branding/controls are preserved

However, YouTube can and does remove or restrict videos, change embed permissions, or place ads before embedded videos. An app that relies on embedded YouTube videos would be dependent on continued availability of those specific videos.

Direct linking to YouTube is always permissible. Deep-linking to specific playlist pages or video pages is standard practice (every school district does it).

## What This Means for a Product

### Habecker as Competitive Context

A math companion app for parents needs to be aware that Habecker's free YouTube library already exists and is already how most parents who seek homework help find it. The question for product design is: what does a product offer that 10 minutes of searching for "Eureka Math Grade 3 Module 1 Lesson 7" on YouTube doesn't?

Possible answers:
- Contextual delivery (surfacing the right video at the right moment, based on what the student is working on today)
- Integration with paper-based work (a camera-based app that identifies the specific problem and finds the relevant video)
- Better navigation / less friction than YouTube's search
- Support beyond re-watching the lesson (e.g., practice problems, alternative explanations, parent coaching)
- Coverage of Eureka Math Squared (the newer curriculum), which Habecker has not yet covered

### Partnership Potential

Habecker is a credible, nationally-known educator with relationships in the Eureka Math community. His EMBARC co-founder relationship and conference speaking suggest he is plugged in to the national Eureka teacher network.

If a product wanted to partner with him:
- There is no commercial arrangement to navigate around (he charges nothing)
- He is reachable via dhabecker@gmail.com and @dhabecker on Twitter
- His current role at MCOE means any formal partnership might require district approval, but his EMBARC and YouTube work appears to be independent of his employment
- He would be an ideal advisor, beta tester, or ambassador for a product serving the Eureka Math teacher/parent community

### Embedding vs. Licensing

If the goal is to use his videos inside an app:
- **Linking out:** Always permissible; zero friction
- **Embedding via YouTube iframe:** Technically permissible under YouTube ToS; no cost but carries YouTube branding, potential ads, and dependency on his channel remaining up
- **Licensing video content directly:** Would require Habecker's permission; he would own rights to his own teaching (the Eureka Math curriculum content itself is OER-licensed, but his specific video recordings are his intellectual property); there is no evidence he has ever licensed them

Given the purely labor-of-love character of his work, a request for permission to embed or reference might receive a very open response — or might get no response at all if he's busy. There is no commercial negotiation to conduct because there is no money involved.
